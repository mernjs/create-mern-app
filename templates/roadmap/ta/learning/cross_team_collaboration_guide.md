# How to Work Well With Other Teams
### A Simple Step-by-Step Guide

---

## Why Does This Matter?

When teams don't talk to each other, things go wrong.

Work gets repeated. Deadlines get missed. People get confused about who is doing what.

When teams work **together well**, things are faster, easier, and better for everyone.

---

## The 8 Steps

---

## Step 1 — Agree on WHY You Are Working Together

Before doing any work, every team needs to answer these 3 questions:

- **What problem are we trying to solve?**
- **What does success look like?**
- **What is everyone responsible for?**

Write the answers down in one simple document. Share it with everyone.

> 💡 **Think of it like this:** Before a road trip, you agree on the destination. Same idea here.

**You are done with this step when:**
Everyone on every team has read and agreed to the shared goal.

---

## Step 2 — Decide Who is in Charge of What

Too many people making decisions = nothing gets done.

So, pick:

**One Lead Person** — Someone who makes sure the project keeps moving. They don't do all the work. They just make sure decisions get made and problems get solved.

**One Contact Per Team** — Each team picks one person to speak for them in cross-team meetings.

**A Simple Ownership Table** — For every task, write down:

| Task | Who Does It | Who Approves It | Who Needs to Know |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design the screen | Design team | Product Lead | Engineering team |
| Write the code | Engineering team | Tech Lead | QA team |

This way, no one ever says "I didn't know that was my job."

**You are done with this step when:**
Every task has a clear owner.

---

## Step 3 — Agree on How You Will Talk to Each Other

Most team problems are actually **communication problems**.

Agree on these things before you start:

**Which tool for what:**

| Situation | Use This |
|---|---|
| Quick question | Slack / Teams message |
| Important update for everyone | Group channel |
| Planning and documents | Shared doc (Google Docs / Notion) |
| Big decisions | Email |

**How fast to reply:**
- Normal message → reply within 4 hours
- Email → reply within 24 hours
- Urgent message → reply within 1 hour

**Always write down decisions.** After any meeting or chat where a decision is made, someone writes it here:

```
Decision: [what was decided]
Who decided: [name]
Date: [date]
Reason: [why]
```

> 💡 **Think of it like this:** If it's not written down, it didn't happen.

**You are done with this step when:**
Everyone knows which tool to use and how fast to reply.

---

## Step 4 — Set Up Your Shared Workspace

Create **one place** where everything lives.

A simple folder structure works great:

```
📁 Project Name
├── 📄 Our Goal
├── 📄 Who Does What
├── 📄 Decisions Log
├── 📄 Meeting Notes
└── 📁 Each Team's Work
```

**Set a regular meeting schedule:**

| Meeting | How Often | How Long | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick check-in | Every week | 30 minutes | Blockers and updates |
| Detailed review | Every 2 weeks | 1 hour | Deep dive into progress |
| Full team update | Every month | 30 minutes | Big picture check |

**Tip:** Before scheduling a meeting, ask — "Can this be a message instead?" Fewer meetings = more time to actually work.

**You are done with this step when:**
Everyone knows where to find information and when the next meeting is.

---

## Step 5 — Track Progress Together

Set **one shared goal** that everyone is working toward. For example:

> *"Launch the new feature by end of October, with 80% of users completing setup successfully."*

Then use a simple weekly status update:

- 🟢 **On track** — Everything is going well
- 🟡 **At risk** — Something might go wrong soon
- 🔴 **Blocked** — We need help right now

Put this somewhere everyone can see it. Update it every week.

When a team hits a milestone, **celebrate it together** — and make sure every team gets credit.

**You are done with this step when:**
Anyone on any team can check the status in 30 seconds.

---

## Step 6 — Handle Disagreements Calmly

Teams will disagree. That's normal. The key is fixing it fast.

**3-step process when teams disagree:**

1. The team contacts try to sort it out themselves — within 24 hours
2. If that doesn't work, the Lead Person steps in — within 48 hours
3. If still stuck, go to a manager — within 72 hours

**When talking through a disagreement, use this language:**

| Don't say | Say this instead |
|---|---|
| "Your team messed up" | "Something broke in the handoff — let's fix it" |
| "That's not our problem" | "Let's figure out who owns this" |
| "You never tell us anything" | "I've been missing updates on X — can we set something up?" |

> 💡 **Remember:** The goal is to fix the problem, not to win the argument.

**You are done with this step when:**
Everyone knows the steps to follow when a disagreement comes up.

---

## Step 7 — Build Trust Between Teams

Trust makes everything easier. Here's how to build it:

**At the start:**
Spend 30 minutes in a "get to know you" call. Teams work better when people feel like humans, not just names on a screen.

**Every week:**
Do what you said you would do. If you can't, say so early — don't wait until the deadline.

**Always:**
When your team succeeds with help from another team, say so out loud. Thank them in meetings. Tag them in messages. Share the credit.

**Every few months:**
Ask each other 3 simple questions:
- What is working well between our teams?
- What is frustrating?
- What one thing could we change to make things better?

> 💡 **Trust is built in small moments.** Showing up, following through, and saying thank you — these things add up.

**You are done with this step when:**
Teams feel comfortable raising problems early instead of hiding them.

---

## Step 8 — Look Back and Get Better

At the end of every project or big milestone, hold a short review meeting.

Ask these questions together:

- Did we hit our goal?
- What slowed us down?
- What should we do differently next time?
- Who deserves a shout-out for great teamwork?

Write down the answers. Use them to improve the next time you work together.

Over time, you will build a **"how we work together" guide** that saves every new team from making the same mistakes.

**You are done with this step when:**
You have written notes from the review that you will actually use next time.

---

## Quick Checklist — Before You Start Any Cross-Team Project

Use this to make sure you have everything in place:

- [ ] Shared goal written and agreed by everyone
- [ ] One Lead Person named
- [ ] One contact person named per team
- [ ] Ownership table filled in
- [ ] Communication rules agreed
- [ ] Shared project folder created
- [ ] Meeting schedule set
- [ ] Progress tracker ready

## One-Page Summary

| Step | What You Do | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Agree on the goal | Write down the shared purpose | Everyone is pointing the same way |
| 2. Decide who owns what | Pick a lead and fill in the ownership table | No confusion about responsibility |
| 3. Agree on communication | Choose tools and reply times | Fewer misunderstandings |
| 4. Set up your workspace | One folder, regular meetings | Everything is easy to find |
| 5. Track progress | Shared status with 🟢🟡🔴 | Problems are caught early |
| 6. Handle disagreements | 3-step process | Conflicts get fixed, not ignored |
| 7. Build trust | Follow through, share credit | Teams actually help each other |
| 8. Review and improve | End-of-project check-in | Each project gets better |

---

> **The simple truth:** Good cross-team collaboration isn't magic. It's just making sure everyone knows the goal, knows their role, and feels safe speaking up. Do those three things and most problems take care of themselves.
