## 1. Pre-Application Prep
Before you even apply — lay the groundwork so you're ready to move fast once things start.

- [ ] Update resume (tailored keywords matching the JD)
- [ ] Update LinkedIn profile (headline, summary, skills endorsements)
- [ ] Prepare a portfolio / GitHub with clean, documented projects
- [ ] Research target companies and shortlist roles
- [ ] Set up a professional email signature and voicemail greeting
- [ ] Keep 2–3 professional references ready with updated contact info

---

## 2. Initial HR Call & Job Role/JD Discussion
A 20–30 min call that covers both basic fit **and** the actual role/JD — usually one conversation with a recruiter or hiring manager.

**Basic fit:**
- [ ] 60-second "Tell me about yourself" pitch
- [ ] Current CTC, expected CTC, and negotiation range
- [ ] Notice period and earliest joining date
- [ ] Reason for job change (positive framing)
- [ ] Location/relocation/remote-work preferences
- [ ] Basic company research (what they do, size, founded year)

**Role & JD discussion:**
- [ ] Line-by-line breakdown of the JD, mapped to your experience
- [ ] 2–3 STAR stories relevant to the role's key responsibilities
- [ ] Clarify ambiguous JD points (team, tech stack, day-to-day work)
- [ ] Understand reporting structure and growth path
- [ ] Questions to ask (team size, interview process/stages, timelines, role priorities in first 6 months)

---

## 3. Online Test / Assessment
Coding tests, aptitude tests, or domain-specific assessments.

- [ ] Identify test type: coding (DSA), aptitude, or role-specific (SQL, Excel, case study)
- [ ] Practice on LeetCode, HackerRank, CodeSignal, GeeksforGeeks
- [ ] Revise core DSA: arrays, strings, linked lists, trees, graphs, DP, recursion
- [ ] Time-boxed mock tests for speed and accuracy
- [ ] Check test format in advance (proctored? IDE allowed? negative marking?)
- [ ] Stable internet, webcam, quiet environment ready

---

## 4. 1st Round Technical Interview
Tests fundamentals, problem-solving, and often includes live coding.

**Core prep:**
- [ ] CS fundamentals: DSA, OOP, DBMS, OS basics
- [ ] Language-specific concepts (deep dive on your primary language)
- [ ] Common conceptual questions (REST APIs, SQL joins, framework internals, etc.)
- [ ] Time/space complexity explanations for your solutions

**Live coding on shared screen (common in this round):**
- [ ] Test your setup beforehand — internet, webcam, mic, screen-share on the platform used (Zoom, CoderPad, HackerRank CodePair, etc.)
- [ ] Practice in a plain/minimal editor, not your full IDE with auto-complete
- [ ] Clarify requirements out loud before coding — don't code in silence
- [ ] Think out loud: explain approach, assumptions, edge cases as you go
- [ ] Start brute-force if needed, then optimize while narrating trade-offs
- [ ] Trace through sample inputs manually before saying "done"
- [ ] Do a couple of mock live-coding sessions beforehand (with a friend or on Pramp/interviewing.io) to get comfortable being watched

---

## 5. 2nd Round Technical Interview
Deeper technical round — advanced problem-solving, your projects, and system design.

**Core prep:**
- [ ] Advanced DSA problems and edge cases
- [ ] System design basics: scalability, caching, database design (for experienced roles)
- [ ] Debugging/troubleshooting scenario questions
- [ ] Trade-off discussions (SQL vs NoSQL, monolith vs microservices, etc.)
- [ ] Domain-specific tools/frameworks from the JD

**Project deep-dive (a major part of this round):**
- [ ] Pick your 2–3 strongest/most relevant projects and know them cold
- [ ] For each: problem statement, your specific role, architecture, tech choices and why
- [ ] A real challenge you faced and how you solved it
- [ ] Trade-offs considered and rejected alternatives
- [ ] Impact/results — metrics, performance gains, business value
- [ ] What you'd improve if rebuilding it today
- [ ] Be ready to sketch the architecture live if asked

**If live coding happens again in this round:** follow the same shared-screen prep as Round 1 (setup, thinking out loud, clean readable code, testing before declaring done).

---

## 6. CTO / Managerial Round
Focus shifts to leadership, ownership, and culture fit.

- [ ] Leadership/ownership stories (STAR): conflict resolution, mentoring, decisions under pressure
- [ ] High-level technical vision questions (architecture choices, scaling challenges)
- [ ] Team collaboration and handling disagreements
- [ ] Business impact of past work (metrics, outcomes, ROI)
- [ ] Career goals aligned with company direction
- [ ] Questions about company vision, tech roadmap, team culture

---

## 7. Client Interview
Common in service-based/consulting companies — assesses client-readiness and trust.

- [ ] Polished, jargon-free communication
- [ ] Understanding of the client's business domain (research beforehand if shared)
- [ ] Scenario questions ("How would you handle a client escalation?")
- [ ] Professional presentation — tone, punctuality, camera etiquette
- [ ] Clarifying questions about client expectations and engagement model

---

## 8. Final Round HR Discussion, Offer & Joining
The last HR conversation — covers final fit, the offer itself, and everything needed to actually join.

**Final discussion:**
- [ ] Salary negotiation numbers (minimum acceptable and ideal)
- [ ] Benefits/perks questions (insurance, PF, bonus, WFH policy)
- [ ] Notice period negotiation
- [ ] Reconfirm joining date flexibility
- [ ] Probation period and appraisal cycle questions
- [ ] Handle competing offers professionally

**Offer & negotiation:**
- [ ] Review offer letter thoroughly (CTC breakup, fixed vs variable, bonus, ESOPs)
- [ ] Compare against market benchmarks (Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, Levels.fyi)
- [ ] Negotiate professionally — justify with market data and your value-add
- [ ] Get everything in writing before resigning from current job

**Background verification:**
- [ ] Keep past employment documents ready (payslips, relieving letter, Form 16)
- [ ] Ensure education certificates are accessible
- [ ] Inform past employers/references about potential verification calls
- [ ] Address any employment gaps proactively and honestly

**Joining & first 90 days:**
- [ ] Serve notice period professionally — complete knowledge transfer (KT)
- [ ] Collect relieving letter, experience letter, F&F settlement details
- [ ] Complete new company's joining formalities (documents, ID proofs, bank details)
- [ ] Plan logistics (commute, remote setup, equipment)
- [ ] Set clear 30-60-90 day goals with your manager once you join
- [ ] Seek feedback proactively and build relationships early

---

## 📌 General Tips
- Research the company before every round
- Keep a consistent narrative across all rounds
- Send a polite thank-you note after each round
- Track your interview pipeline (company, round, date, feedback)
- Stay calm — treat each round as a two-way conversation
